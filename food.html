Downtown L.A. is home to various types of cuisine from all over the world. From freshly made artisan pasta in skid row to fresh hamburgers in Chintown. There is a surprise behind every corner. Here are some suggestions on great places to eat in the area


Roy Choi has been able to grow his culinary empire within the last several years, translating into a hotel and other restaurants around the state. Chego in Chinatown is where Choi brings homestyle Korean cuisine to the masses.




Another stop in downtown if you are hungry is the Grand Central Market. You can indulge from beer to sweets to breakfast to coffee, all under one roof. With L.A. staples like EggSlut and Golden Road, this is a definite stop to recharge on your rides.
